Ever wondered how the different chips inside an iPhone are made? How much they cost? Why solar panels doting US-101 look different than ones on the rooftop? Learn about these topics and much more in Nanomanufacturing a course being offered by EE department at Stanford. Topics include patterning, 3D chips and packaging, display and LED technology etc.

Learn about fundamentals of nanomanufacturing technology and applications. Topics include recent developments in process technology, lithography and patterning. Technology for FinFET transistors, NAND flash and 3D chips. Manufacturing of thin film and crystalline solar cells.

Logistics

This course makes a use of an adapted flip-classroom model, whereby approximately half of the class time is devoted to lectures which are supplemented by short videos of 10-12 minutes each to develop the fundamentals. Remainder of the class time will be devoted to discussing the recent trends, success stories and invited talks consisting of speakers from startups, bigger companies and venture capital industry actively engaged in the field of nanomanufacturing.
